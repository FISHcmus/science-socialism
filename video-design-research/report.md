## Apple Keynote Inspired

*Ultra-clean, large text on solid backgrounds, dramatic reveals*

### Color Palette

- **primary**: #000000 (pure black for hero text and dramatic slides)
- **secondary**: #1D4ED8 (Apple-style blue for accents and links)
- **accent**: #7C3AED (purple gradient accent for special moments)
- **surface**: #FFFFFF (pure white for light slides)
- **surface_dark**: #000000 (pure black for dramatic contrast slides)
- **surface_gradient**: linear-gradient(135deg, #667EEA, #764BA2) (hero gradient backgrounds)
- **text**: #1F2937 (dark gray for body text on white)
- **text_on_dark**: #FFFFFF (white text on dark/gradient slides)

### Typography

- **heading_font**: Be Vietnam Pro ExtraBold (800) / Black (900) at very large sizes
- **body_font**: Be Vietnam Pro Regular (400) / Medium (500)
- **size_scale**:
  - **hero_statement**: 72-96px (single-line dramatic reveals)
  - **section_title**: 56-64px
  - **section_label**: 32px uppercase
  - **card_heading**: 40px
  - **body_text**: 28-32px
  - **citation**: 24px
- **weights_used**: 400, 500, 700, 800, 900
- **vietnamese_diacritics**:
  > Good - Be Vietnam Pro handles large sizes well; at 72-96px hero sizes, diacritics are massive and crystal clear. Risk: Black (900) weight may cause diacritics to feel heavy/crowded at smaller sizes.

### Animation Style

- **approach**: Dramatic reveals - elements appear with impact, scale transitions, cinematic pacing
- **entrance_pattern**: Scale from 0.8 to 1.0 with opacity, or slide-up from below frame. Key moments use 'appear from nothing' with brief black pause between slides.
- **spring_config**: Damping 15-20, mass 1, stiffness 150 (snappy, decisive - no wobble)
- **motion_budget**: Medium-high - Apple keynotes are motion-heavy but each animation serves a purpose (reveal, emphasis, transition)

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_approach**: SPLIT PROBLEM: White slides with dark text = excellent. Black slides with white text = problematic on classroom projectors.
- **min_font_sizes**: Hero text at 72-96px is massively readable. Body at 28-32px standard. The issue is not size but contrast on dark slides.
- **color_washout**:
  > WARNING: Dark/black background slides will appear as washed-out gray on most classroom projectors. White text on projected gray is significantly less readable than dark text on projected white. Blue and purple accents lose vibrancy on projectors.
- **line_weight**: Clean lines, no thin decorative elements - good for projection

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Medium (split)
- **details**:
  > This style inherently requires BOTH light and dark slides for its dramatic effect. Light slides project beautifully. Dark slides suffer from projector black-level limitations - classroom projectors (typically 2500-4000 lumens) render black as medium gray, destroying the dramatic contrast that defines the Apple keynote look. Gradients lose their subtlety. The signature 'black slide with white text reveal' becomes 'gray slide with white text' - underwhelming rather than dramatic.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **font_suitability**: Good - Be Vietnam Pro at hero sizes (72-96px) renders diacritics impressively; the large scale makes Vietnamese text a visual feature
- **line_height_needs**: line-height 1.3-1.4 for hero text (tighter for impact), 1.5-1.6 for body
- **min_size_for_clarity**: Not an issue - this style's minimum sizes exceed most others' maximums

### Cultural Fit

- **alignment**: Weak - Apple keynote aesthetic is strongly associated with Silicon Valley corporate culture
- **details**:
  > The dramatic reveal style mimics tech product launches ('One more thing...'). For Vietnamese political science content about national unity, this feels like using a product launch template for an essay. The black/white drama doesn't map to any Vietnamese cultural convention. Blue/purple accents have no Vietnamese political meaning.
- **risk**: Teacher may see it as impressive but question its appropriateness for academic political content. Could feel like form over substance.

### Cultural Semiotics

- **black_slides**: Drama, premium, tech luxury, Steve Jobs - zero Vietnamese resonance
- **white_slides**: Clean, modern, authoritative - culturally neutral
- **blue_purple_gradient**: Innovation, technology, premium branding - corporate Western semiotics
- **dramatic_reveals**: Product announcement, surprise, spectacle - entertainment/commercial semiotics
- **overall**: Every semiotic signal points to Silicon Valley product culture; actively conflicts with socialist/academic subject matter

### Teacher Perception Signal

- **signal**: Technically impressive but culturally disconnected
- **details**:
  > Teacher recognizes the Apple keynote style (everyone has seen Apple events). This communicates high production skill but may raise questions about whether students focused on looking cool rather than engaging with the material. In Vietnamese academic context, this aesthetic may feel imported and inauthentic.
- **effort_perception**: High effort recognized - the dramatic animations and clean execution clearly required work

### Rubric Alignment

- **format_15pct**: Strong - dramatic and polished format that demonstrates production quality
- **theory_35pct**:
  > Weak - Apple keynote style uses very few words per slide (1-3 key phrases), which makes presenting dense socialist theory extremely difficult. Would need 3x more slides to cover same theory content.
- **application_50pct**:
  > Medium - data can be presented dramatically (big number reveals, chart animations) but narrative application content suffers from the sparse-text constraint

### Content Density Capacity

- **words_per_frame**: 20-50 words - deliberately sparse; Apple style shows one idea per slide at massive scale
- **data_viz_support**: Good for individual stats (big number reveals) but poor for complex multi-variable charts or comparative data
- **theory_density**: Very low - the style fundamentally conflicts with dense academic text; theory would require many more slides/frames

### Production Effort

- **migration_cost**:
  > High - requires complete redesign. Need to build dark-mode slide variants, hero text components, gradient backgrounds, dramatic transition system. Current card-based editorial layout is fundamentally different from the one-idea-per-slide Apple approach.
- **component_changes**:
  > Major - need HeroText, DramaticReveal, GradientBackground, BlackSlide, WhiteSlide components; existing GlassPanel/IconGrid/FlowChart don't fit the aesthetic; need to restructure content flow for more slides with less content each

### Remotion Implementation Complexity

- **css_needs**: Moderate - gradients, large text styling, slide transitions are standard CSS
- **threejs_usage**: Could keep 3D for dramatic reveals but style is fundamentally 2D; 3D is optional decorative layer
- **render_performance**: Medium - more frames needed (content spread across more slides), gradient rendering, scale animations
- **overall**: Medium-high complexity, primarily due to content restructuring rather than technical CSS challenges

**Distraction Level**: Medium - dramatic reveals draw attention to the reveal moment itself rather than the content; the style IS the distraction in many ways

**Differentiation**: High - no student group presents political science like an Apple keynote; extremely memorable but risky

### Temporal Coherence

- **consistency**: Medium - alternating between black and white slides creates rhythm but the dramatic pacing is hard to sustain for 13.5 minutes without fatigue
- **section_transitions**: Good - black pause slides between sections are natural transition points
- **speaker_differentiation**: Difficult - the stark black/white palette leaves little room for speaker color coding; would need subtle accent color per speaker

### Dark Vs Light Mode Viability

> Requires BOTH dark and light modes alternating for full effect. Light-only version loses the dramatic reveals. Dark-only version is a projector disaster. The hybrid requirement is this style's core vulnerability for projector presentation.

### Precedent

- **examples**:
  - Apple WWDC and product launch keynotes - the definitive reference
  - Google I/O keynote presentations - similar dramatic style with Material Design
  - Tesla product reveal presentations - dark backgrounds, dramatic text
  - TED and TEDx presentations by tech speakers
  - SlidesCarnival Apple-inspired templates
- **notes**:
  > All precedents are corporate product launches and tech conferences. No academic or political science precedents exist for this style. The style was designed for dimmed auditoriums with high-end projectors/LED walls, not classroom projectors.

**Uncertain fields**:
- wcag_contrast_score

## Bento

*Warm peach + soft blue on cream, modular grid cards, trendy tech*

### Color Palette

- **primary**: #F97316 (warm peach-orange for primary accents)
- **secondary**: #60A5FA (soft blue for secondary accents and data)
- **accent**: #FBBF24 (warm yellow for highlights)
- **surface**: #FFFBF0 (warm cream background)
- **surface_alt**: #FFFFFF (pure white for card interiors)
- **card_border**: #E5E7EB (light gray, subtle card separation)
- **text**: #1F2937 (gray-800 for body text)
- **text_secondary**: #6B7280 (gray-500 for secondary text)

### Typography

- **heading_font**: Be Vietnam Pro Bold/SemiBold (or Inter/Plus Jakarta Sans for full Bento aesthetic)
- **body_font**: Be Vietnam Pro Regular/Medium
- **size_scale**:
  - **section_label**: 32px
  - **section_title**: 48-52px
  - **card_heading**: 36px
  - **body_text**: 28-30px
  - **stat_number**: 64-72px (hero numbers in bento tiles)
  - **citation**: 24px
- **weights_used**: 400, 500, 600, 700
- **vietnamese_diacritics**: Good with Be Vietnam Pro; Inter has acceptable but not purpose-built Vietnamese support; Plus Jakarta Sans adequate but not optimized

### Animation Style

- **approach**: Playful, staggered card reveals - each bento tile animates in with slight delay, scale-up with spring
- **entrance_pattern**: Cards scale from 0.9 to 1.0 with fade-in, staggered by 3-5 frames per card; parallax subtle hover effects
- **spring_config**: Damping 10-12, mass 0.6, stiffness 100 (slightly bouncy, playful feel)
- **motion_budget**: Medium-high - the grid layout invites animation of individual tiles, which adds visual interest but risks being distracting

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_approach**: Dark text on cream/white backgrounds; pastel accents (peach, soft blue) may wash out on projectors
- **min_font_sizes**: Body 28px, headings 36px+ adequate but stat numbers at 64-72px are the real visual anchors
- **color_washout**:
  > WARNING: Soft blue (#60A5FA) and warm peach (#F97316) at reduced saturation may lose identity when projected. Peach-orange is more resilient than soft blue. Cream background (#FFFBF0) projects cleanly.
- **line_weight**: Card borders typically 1px with shadow - thin borders WILL disappear on projectors; need to increase to 2px solid

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **text_on_surface**: #1F2937 on #FFFBF0 = 14.1:1 (AAA pass)
- **text_on_white_card**: #1F2937 on #FFFFFF = 15.4:1 (AAA pass)
- **peach_on_cream**: #F97316 on #FFFBF0 = 3.1:1 (FAIL for small text, AA pass for large text only)
- **blue_on_cream**: #60A5FA on #FFFBF0 = 2.8:1 (FAIL for text - use only for decorative/icon purposes)
- **overall**: Body text passes AAA; accent colors fail for functional text and must be limited to large headings, icons, or decorative elements

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Medium-low
- **details**:
  > The soft, pastel nature of Bento palettes is designed for high-quality screens (Retina displays, modern monitors), not projectors. Soft blue washes out significantly. Peach-orange loses warmth and may read as generic orange. Card shadows (typical in Bento) become invisible on projectors. The cream background itself is fine. Grid gaps and card separation rely on subtle shadows that projectors eliminate - need solid borders instead.

### Cultural Fit

- **alignment**: Weak - Bento is a Japanese-inspired tech aesthetic with no Vietnamese cultural resonance
- **details**:
  > The Bento grid style is associated with Apple WWDC, Silicon Valley product pages, and tech startups. Peach and soft blue evoke modern SaaS dashboards, not Vietnamese academic or political content. For a topic on national unity (đại đoàn kết toàn dân tộc), this palette and style feel disconnected from the subject matter.
- **risk**:
  > High - teacher may perceive a mismatch between trendy tech aesthetics and serious political/academic content; could seem like prioritizing style over substance

### Cultural Semiotics

- **peach_orange**: Tech warmth, Silicon Valley friendliness, startup energy - no Vietnamese cultural meaning
- **soft_blue**: Trust, technology, calmness - generic corporate/tech semiotics
- **cream**: Warmth, approachability - neutral
- **bento_grid**: Japanese lunch box organization metaphor, adopted by Apple and tech companies - foreign cultural reference
- **overall**: Semiotics point entirely to tech industry; zero Vietnamese or socialist/political symbolism

### Teacher Perception Signal

- **signal**: Technically skilled but potentially misaligned priorities
- **details**:
  > Teacher sees students with strong design/tech skills (positive) but may question why a political science presentation looks like a tech product page (negative). The trendiness could be interpreted as caring more about aesthetics than academic content.
- **effort_perception**: High technical effort recognized, but cultural appropriateness may be questioned

### Rubric Alignment

- **format_15pct**: Strong for format points - modular grid is visually organized and impressive
- **theory_35pct**: Weak - bento tiles encourage bite-sized info chunks that may fragment theoretical arguments; dense theory doesn't fit naturally in small cards
- **application_50pct**: Medium - stat numbers and data viz work well in bento tiles, but narrative application content is harder to present in modular chunks

### Content Density Capacity

- **words_per_frame**: 60-90 words - bento layout distributes content across multiple small cards, reducing per-card density
- **data_viz_support**: Good for stats and numbers (hero stat tiles), medium for complex charts (space constraints in grid cells)
- **theory_density**: Low-medium - the modular nature fragments long theoretical arguments; better for bullet points than paragraphs

### Production Effort

- **migration_cost**:
  > High - requires complete redesign from current card-based editorial layout to modular grid system. Need to implement CSS Grid with variable-size tiles, rethink information architecture per frame, redesign all section components.
- **component_changes**:
  > Major - need new BentoGrid container, BentoTile components with size variants (1x1, 2x1, 1x2, 2x2), stat tiles, icon tiles. Existing GlassPanel/SectionTitle need significant rework.

### Remotion Implementation Complexity

- **css_needs**: Complex - CSS Grid with named areas, responsive tile sizing, shadow/blur effects (though shadows should be replaced with borders for projector)
- **threejs_usage**: 3D background should be removed - Bento style is flat/2D by nature
- **render_performance**: Medium - multiple animated tiles per frame increases render computation; staggered animations need careful frame counting
- **overall**: High complexity - fundamentally different layout paradigm from current codebase

**Distraction Level**: Medium-high - the grid animation and multiple tiles competing for attention can distract from content, especially with staggered reveals

**Differentiation**: High - no other student group will use Bento-style layouts for political science; stands out dramatically but may stand out for wrong reasons

### Temporal Coherence

- **consistency**: Medium - grid layout provides structural consistency but tile arrangements will vary significantly per section, potentially feeling inconsistent
- **section_transitions**: Challenging - transitioning between different grid configurations may feel jarring
- **speaker_differentiation**: Can assign tile colors per speaker but this fragments the already-complex grid further

### Dark Vs Light Mode Viability

> Primarily light mode. Bento can work dark (Apple WWDC style) but pastel accents lose their character on dark backgrounds. Light cream is the canonical Bento surface.

### Precedent

- **examples**:
  - Apple WWDC 2023-2024 feature slides - dark Bento grid with product feature tiles
  - Linear.app landing page - Bento-style feature grid
  - Notion feature marketing pages - tinted Bento cards grouped by category
  - Vercel/Next.js landing pages - modular grid layouts
  - bentogrids.com - curated gallery of Bento grid implementations
- **notes**:
  > All precedents are from tech/product contexts. No examples found of Bento style used for academic, political, or Vietnamese cultural presentations. This would be a first-of-its-kind application.

- vietnamese_diacritics_rendering

## Chalkboard/University

*Dark green or navy bg, handwritten accents, academic warmth*

### Color Palette

- **description**: Classic chalkboard aesthetic with warm academic tones. Two primary variants: dark green (traditional chalkboard) and navy (modern academic).
- **background_green**: #1B3B2A
- **background_navy**: #1A1F3B
- **chalk_white**: #E8E4D8
- **chalk_yellow**: #F5D76E
- **chalk_pink**: #E8A0BF
- **chalk_blue**: #7EC8E3
- **chalk_orange**: #E8A87C
- **text_primary**: #E8E4D8
- **text_accent**: #F5D76E
- **board_frame**: #5C3A1E
- **note**: Colors simulate chalk on slate. All 'chalk' colors are slightly muted/dusty rather than pure, mimicking real chalk texture.

### Typography

- **recommended_families**:
  - Indie Flower or Patrick Hand (handwritten headers)
  - Be Vietnam Pro (body text for readability)
  - Nanum Pen Script (chalk-like handwriting alternative)
- **size_scale**:
  > Headers in handwritten font: 56-72px. Body text in sans-serif: 32-40px. Chalk annotations: 24-28px. Must be larger than normal due to low-contrast background.
- **weights**: Handwritten fonts are single-weight. Body sans-serif uses Regular (400) and SemiBold (600).
- **vietnamese_diacritics**:
  > CRITICAL WEAKNESS: Most handwritten/chalk fonts have poor or no Vietnamese diacritic support. Indie Flower, Patrick Hand, and similar fonts do not handle stacked diacritics (uong, uoi) correctly. Must fall back to sans-serif (Be Vietnam Pro) for any Vietnamese text, which undermines the handwritten aesthetic.

### Animation Style

- **approach**: Chalk-drawing effect: text and diagrams appear as if being written/drawn by hand. Whiteboard animation style.
- **entrance_patterns**: Stroke-by-stroke text reveal (chalk writing simulation), hand-drawn line animations for diagrams and charts, eraser-wipe transitions between sections
- **motion_budget**: High - the writing animation IS the visual identity. Every text element ideally has a chalk-writing entrance, which is animation-intensive.
- **note**: Authentic chalk animation requires SVG path animation or canvas drawing, which is computationally expensive in Remotion

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**:
  > Problematic. Chalk white (#E8E4D8) on dark green (#1B3B2A) achieves ~8:1, which is good in theory. BUT projectors render dark backgrounds as washed-out gray, dramatically reducing effective contrast.
- **minimum_font_sizes**: Must use 32px+ for body text due to the inherent contrast challenges of dark backgrounds on projectors.
- **color_washout_resistance**:
  > POOR. Dark green background becomes muddy medium gray on projectors. Chalk white text loses its warmth and becomes indistinguishable from the gray background at distance. This is the style's fundamental weakness for projector presentation.
- **critical_issue**:
  > Dark backgrounds are the opposite of projector-optimized design. Projectors produce light, so whites are bright but darks become gray. A chalkboard that looks green on a monitor will look gray on a projector.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **chalk_on_green**: 8.2:1 (#E8E4D8 on #1B3B2A) - AAA pass on screen
- **yellow_on_green**: 6.1:1 (#F5D76E on #1B3B2A) - AA pass
- **pink_on_green**: 4.8:1 (#E8A0BF on #1B3B2A) - AA pass for normal text
- **degradation_note**:
  > CRITICAL: With 20-30% projector degradation, effective contrast drops to 4-5:1 for white and below 3:1 for colored chalks. Yellow and pink chalk become nearly invisible on washed-out gray background.

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Poor
- **strengths**: None significant for projector use. The style is designed for screens, not projection.
- **weaknesses**:
  > Dark background becomes gray. Chalk texture details disappear entirely. Colored chalk (yellow, pink, blue) loses distinction. Handwritten text at small sizes becomes illegible. The entire visual identity depends on the green-to-chalk contrast that projectors destroy.
- **recommendation**:
  > If using this style, invert to light mode: cream/off-white background with dark green text and accents. This preserves the academic warmth while being projector-safe, but loses the signature chalkboard look.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**: Poor for handwritten fonts, adequate for sans-serif fallback
- **line_height_needs**:
  > Handwritten fonts require extra line-height (1.6-1.8x) due to irregular baselines and ascender/descender variation. Vietnamese diacritics compound this problem.
- **minimum_safe_size**: 36px+ for handwritten fonts with Vietnamese text to prevent diacritic collisions. 28px for sans-serif fallback.
- **critical_issue**: Handwritten fonts + Vietnamese diacritics + dark background + projector = quadruple readability penalty

### Cultural Fit

- **rating**: Moderate
- **analysis**:
  > Chalkboard evokes education and teaching tradition, which is appropriate for an academic assignment. Vietnamese students and teachers associate chalkboards with classroom learning. However, the style feels nostalgic/retro rather than forward-looking, which may not suit a topic about building national unity for the future. The dark green could inadvertently evoke military/revolution aesthetics.
- **adaptation_needed**: Would benefit from incorporating Vietnamese cultural elements (lotus, flag colors) as chalk illustrations to connect to the national unity topic

### Cultural Semiotics

- **inherent_symbols**:
  > Chalkboard = education, teaching, tradition, knowledge transfer. Green = nature, growth (positive in Vietnamese culture). Academic cap/gown associations.
- **strength**: Immediately communicates 'this is an educational/academic work' to the teacher
- **weakness**: No inherent Vietnamese national unity symbolism. Must be added through content, not design.

### Teacher Perception Signal

- **signal**:
  > Mixed. The chalkboard style communicates 'we respect the academic tradition' and creates a warm, approachable feel. However, it could also signal 'we used a template' since chalkboard PowerPoint themes are extremely common and often low-effort.
- **risk**:
  > High risk of appearing like a downloaded template rather than original work. Chalkboard themes are among the most overused presentation styles globally.

### Rubric Alignment

- **format_15_percent**: Moderate - unique aesthetic that stands out, but projector readability issues could hurt format scores if text is illegible
- **theory_35_percent**:
  > Moderate - the educational context supports theory presentation. Chalk-drawn diagrams can illustrate theoretical frameworks effectively. But readability constraints limit text density.
- **application_50_percent**:
  > Weak - chalk-drawn charts and data visualizations are charming but imprecise. Statistical data and real-world application examples need clean, readable formatting that conflicts with the handwritten aesthetic.

### Content Density Capacity

- **words_per_frame**: Low to moderate: 40-70 words per frame. Dark background and handwritten elements require larger text, reducing available space.
- **theory_support**: Moderate - can present theory through 'lecture notes' aesthetic, but limited by readability constraints
- **data_viz_support**: Weak - hand-drawn charts are charming but imprecise. Statistical data loses credibility in chalk-drawn format.

### Production Effort

- **migration_from_current**:
  > High. Requires complete visual overhaul: new color scheme (dark bg), handwritten font integration, chalk texture effects, custom SVG animations for chalk-writing effect.
- **estimated_effort**:
  > 4-6 days. Chalk-writing animation system is complex to implement in Remotion. Need custom SVG path animation components. Texture overlays for chalk dust effect.
- **custom_components**: Need: ChalkText component (SVG path reveal), ChalkDiagram (animated line drawing), ChalkboardFrame (decorative border), EraserTransition

### Remotion Implementation Complexity

- **css_needs**: Moderate - texture overlays, noise filters for chalk effect, custom fonts
- **three_js**: Not needed but could be used for chalk particle effects (dust). Would add complexity.
- **render_performance**:
  > High concern - SVG path animation for chalk writing is computationally expensive per frame. 13.5 minutes of chalk animation at 30fps = many SVG calculations.

## Kinetic Typography / Motion-First

*Text as primary visual, animated word reveals, dramatic scale. Good for theory-heavy sections*

### Color Palette

- **primary**: #F7F3EE (warm off-white background for projector)
- **secondary**: #1C1917 (dark text as the primary visual element)
- **accent**: #B91C1C (Vietnam red for emphasis words), #D97706 (amber-gold for highlighted phrases)
- **surface**: Minimal - kinetic typography uses the full frame as canvas, surfaces are implied by text grouping
- **text**: #1C1917 (primary), #B91C1C (emphasis), #78716C (secondary/faded text)
- **notes**:
  > Color palette is intentionally minimal. The typography IS the visual. Color accents are used sparingly to highlight key terms (e.g., 'dai doan ket' in red, statistics in gold).

### Typography

- **font_families**:
  > Be Vietnam Pro (sans-serif) as base. Kinetic typography benefits from weight variety: 300 (light, background words), 400 (body), 700 (bold emphasis), 900 (black, hero words). A single font family with many weights is ideal.
- **size_scale**: Hero word: 120-200px, Key phrase: 56-80px, Supporting text: 36-48px, Detail text: 28-32px. The dramatic size range IS the design.
- **weights**: 300-900 range used expressively. Weight transitions can be animated for emphasis.
- **vietnamese_diacritics_support**:
  > Critical concern at large sizes (120-200px). Be Vietnam Pro handles this well but line-height must be generous (1.5+) to avoid diacritic clipping at hero sizes. Stacked marks on capital letters at 200px need testing.

### Animation Style

- **approach**:
  > Text is the motion. Words fly in, scale up, rotate, fade, split apart. Each word or phrase has its own entrance choreography timed to narration. Spring configs vary per emphasis: hero words use bouncy springs (damping 8, stiffness 80), body text uses subtle fades.
- **entrance_patterns**:
  > Word-by-word reveal (typewriter at phrase level), scale-up from center, slide-in from edges, parallax depth layers, word explosion/assembly, character-by-character build
- **motion_budget**:
  > Very high. Motion IS the content. Every frame has active animation. This is the most animation-heavy style. Budget: 5-15 simultaneous animated elements per frame.

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**:
  > Excellent for hero text (large size compensates for any washout). Problematic for smaller supporting text that may be in motion. Moving text is inherently harder to read than static text.
- **minimum_font_sizes**: Hero: 120px+ (always readable). Supporting: 36px+ (readable if stationary or slow-moving). Detail: 28px+ (must be static or very slow fade-in).
- **color_washout_resistance**:
  > High for primary dark-on-light contrast. The large scale of text means even washed-out projectors render it clearly. Accent colors (red, gold) at large sizes survive washout.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **text_on_background**:
  > Dark text #1C1917 on #F7F3EE: 14.8:1 (AAA). Red accent #B91C1C on #F7F3EE: 5.6:1 (AA). Faded text #78716C on #F7F3EE: 3.8:1 (AA large text only - acceptable at 48px+).
- **recommendation**: Keep faded/secondary text at 48px+ to qualify as 'large text' for WCAG AA compliance.

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: High
- **details**:
  > Large text is inherently projector-resistant. Even with significant color degradation, dark text on light background at 56-200px remains legible from back of classroom. The main risk is during motion: fast-moving text at small sizes may smear on projectors with slow response times.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**:
  > Good but requires careful testing at extreme sizes. At 120-200px hero size, Vietnamese diacritics become very prominent visual elements - this can be a feature (showing the beauty of Vietnamese script) or a problem (diacritics extend far above baseline, requiring extra vertical space). Line-height 1.5+ mandatory at hero sizes.

### Cultural Fit

Vietnamese political vocabulary (dai doan ket, khoi dai doan ket toan dan toc) can be given visual weight through scale and motion.
- **alignment_with_topic**: Medium - serves the educational mission of emphasizing key concepts, but the flashy motion style may feel more 'advertising' than 'academic'

## Neubrutalism / Neo-Brutalist

*Bold primary colors, thick black borders, chunky shadows, flat pop-art. High contrast, projector-safe*

### Color Palette

- **description**:
  > Bold, high-saturation primary colors with thick black outlines. Backgrounds are typically off-white, beige, or a single bold color. Limited palette (3-5 colors) but each color is loud.
- **background**: #F5F0E8
- **background_alt**: #FFE7C2
- **primary_red**: #E63946
- **primary_yellow**: #FFD166
- **primary_blue**: #457B9D
- **accent_green**: #2A9D8F
- **border**: #000000
- **text_primary**: #000000
- **text_on_color**: #000000
- **shadow**: #000000
- **note**: Neubrutalism always uses pure black (#000000) for borders and shadows. No gradients, no transparency, no blur. Colors are flat and opaque.

### Typography

- **recommended_families**:
  - Inter (modern neo-grotesque)
  - Space Grotesk (geometric with personality)
  - Be Vietnam Pro (Vietnamese support + clean geometric feel)
- **size_scale**: Oversized headlines: 72-120px. Body text: 28-36px. Labels/tags: 20-24px. The style embraces extreme size contrast between headings and body.
- **weights**: Black (900) or ExtraBold (800) for headlines. Bold (700) for emphasis. Medium (500) for body. Thin weights are antithetical to the style.
- **vietnamese_diacritics**: Be Vietnam Pro works well. Inter has decent Vietnamese support. The large font sizes used in neubrutalism mean diacritics are rarely a problem.

### Animation Style

- **approach**: Snappy and direct. No easing curves - elements pop in abruptly or slam into position. Spring animations with high stiffness, low damping (bouncy).
- **entrance_patterns**: Hard cut-ins, scale-from-zero pops, sudden rotations (5-15 degrees), cards slamming down with shadow offset animation. No gentle fades.
- **motion_budget**: Moderate to high - animations are brief but impactful. Each element has a punchy entrance. Total animation time per element: 200-400ms.
- **spring_config**: stiffness: 300-500, damping: 10-20 (bouncy). Or no spring at all - just hard transitions.

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**: Excellent. Black text (#000000) on off-white (#F5F0E8) achieves ~16:1. Thick black borders (3-5px) remain visible even on washed-out projectors.
- **minimum_font_sizes**: The style naturally uses large fonts (28px+ body). Thick borders add visual weight that aids readability at distance.
- **color_washout_resistance**:
  > EXCELLENT. The style's defining features (thick black borders, chunky shadows, bold colors, large text) are the exact properties that survive projector degradation best. Even if colors wash out, the black structural elements remain.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **text_on_background**: 16.3:1 (#000000 on #F5F0E8) - AAA pass
- **text_on_yellow**: 15.4:1 (#000000 on #FFD166) - AAA pass
- **text_on_red**: 5.2:1 (#000000 on #E63946) - AA pass for large text
- **degradation_note**:
  > Black borders and shadows provide structural contrast independent of color. Even with severe projector degradation, the layout remains readable because structure is defined by borders, not color alone.

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Excellent - best among all candidates
- **strengths**:
  > Thick black borders (3-5px) remain visible even on the worst projectors. Chunky drop shadows are visible even when blurred. Bold primary colors maintain identity even when desaturated. Large text sizes provide inherent readability margin. The style was practically designed for low-fidelity reproduction.
- **weaknesses**:
  > None significant. The only risk is that very saturated colors (pure red, pure yellow) may shift hue slightly on projectors, but the black structural elements maintain layout integrity.
- **recommendation**: This style is naturally projector-proof. No special accommodations needed.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**: Good to excellent. Large font sizes (28px+ body, 72px+ headers) mean Vietnamese diacritics are never at risk of clipping or collision.
- **line_height_needs**: The style's generous spacing and oversized text naturally accommodate Vietnamese diacritics. Line-height 1.3-1.5x is sufficient.
- **minimum_safe_size**: 28px body text with Be Vietnam Pro - no diacritics issues at this size

## Restorative / Earthy Neutral (2026 Trend)

*Warm neutrals, earthy browns, natural greens, Transformative Teal. Grounded, trustworthy*

### Color Palette

- **primary**: #F7F3EE (warm off-white - already the current theme base, also matches 2026 'Creamy' trend)
- **secondary**: #A67B5B (warm brown / Universal Khaki - Sherwin-Williams 2026 COTY), #78716C (stone gray)
- **accent**: #0D7377 (Transformative Teal - WGSN/Coloro 2026 trend color), #6B7F3E (dusty olive green), #C05621 (terracotta/reddened earth)

  > 2026 color trend is a deliberate move away from bold contrasts toward layered, tonal warmth. Sherwin-Williams Universal Khaki, Pantone Cloud Dancer, and WGSN Transformative Teal are the anchor colors. - **font_families**:

- **approach**:
  > Gentle, organic motion. Slow fades (20-30 frames), subtle parallax, breathing scale (0.98 to 1.02 oscillation). Spring config: damping 25-30, stiffness 40-60 (very gentle, no bounce, like a slow exhale). Nature-inspired easing.

- **color_washout_resistance**:

## Retro-Futurism / Mid-Century Modern

*Geometric shapes, vintage palettes (mustard/teal/coral). Distinctive but culturally disconnected*

- **accent**: #E76F51 (warm coral/burnt orange - tertiary pop)
- **surface**: #FAF3E8 (warm cream/ivory - vintage paper feel)
- **text**: #264653 (deep teal-black - softer than pure black)

  > Classic mid-century palette of mustard, teal, and coral with warm cream background. Additional colors: avocado green (#6B8E23), burnt sienna (#A0522D). The palette is warm and inviting but carries strong 1950s-60s American associations. Retro futurism palettes also include steel gray (#1E1E2F) and bright gold (#F0A400) for more space-age variants.

### Typography

- **headline_font**: Futura or Century Gothic (geometric sans-serif, quintessential mid-century typeface)

- **vietnamese_support**:
  > Futura has limited Vietnamese diacritics support. Century Gothic handles basic Vietnamese but struggles with complex stacked diacritics (ượ, ễ). Be Vietnam Pro for body text ensures readability. Headline font mismatch creates visual tension.
- **notes**:
  > Mid-century typography is defined by geometric sans-serifs (Futura, Avant Garde, Century Gothic). These fonts were designed for Latin scripts and have poor Vietnamese support. The design requires a compromise: use geometric sans for decorative elements only, Be Vietnam Pro for all readable text.

- **notes**:

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Poor

## Slide:ology / Presentation Zen

*Content-driven, one idea per frame, full-bleed images, minimal text*

### Color Palette

- **description**:
  > Presentation Zen does not prescribe a specific color palette. It advocates for restrained color use driven by content and imagery. Typical implementations use:
- **background**: #FFFFFF or full-bleed photography
- **text**: #1A1A1A (near-black) or #FFFFFF (on dark images)
- **accent**: One accent color derived from brand or topic - for Vietnamese national unity, red (#B91C1C) or gold (#D97706) would be natural choices
- **philosophy**: Let the imagery set the color mood. Avoid decorative color that competes with photographs. Maximum 2-3 colors beyond black/white.
- **note**: The style is defined more by what it removes (gradients, multi-color schemes, decorative borders) than what it adds

### Typography

- **recommended_families**:
  - Any clean sans-serif - Be Vietnam Pro ideal for Vietnamese content
  - Garr Reynolds uses Myriad Pro in his books, but advocates for whatever is most readable
- **size_scale**:
  > Extremely large. Presentation Zen advocates for text large enough to read from the back of the room. Typical: headlines 72-120pt equivalent, body text 36-48pt equivalent. At 1920x1080: headline 80-120px, supporting text 40-56px.
- **weights**: Bold for headlines, Regular for minimal body text. Weight contrast replaces color for hierarchy.
- **vietnamese_diacritics**: No specific guidance - style is font-agnostic. Be Vietnam Pro works well with the large sizes advocated.

### Animation Style

- **approach**:
  > Minimal to none. Presentation Zen philosophy treats animation as distraction unless it serves the narrative. Preferred transitions: simple cut or gentle fade.
- **entrance_patterns**: Fade-in for text overlays on images. No bouncing, sliding, or spinning. Content appears, it does not perform.
- **motion_budget**: Very low. A 13.5-minute video in this style might have 5-10 total animated moments, each a simple opacity transition.
- **note**: This is the most animation-conservative style among all candidates

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**:
  > Typically excellent. Large white text on dark photography or dark text on white achieves 10:1+ ratios. Full-bleed images need careful selection to ensure text overlay areas have sufficient contrast.
- **minimum_font_sizes**: This style naturally uses very large fonts (40px+ minimum), far exceeding projector readability requirements.
- **color_washout_resistance**: Photography-dependent. High-quality, high-contrast images resist washout well. Low-contrast or pastel images suffer significantly on projectors.
- **risk**: Text overlaid on busy images can become unreadable on washed-out projectors. Needs semi-transparent overlay behind text.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **typical_combinations**: White (#FFFFFF) on dark overlay (#000000 at 60-70% opacity): 8-12:1 depending on underlying image. Dark text (#1A1A1A) on white (#FFFFFF): 17.6:1.
- **rating**: AA to AAA when executed properly, but highly dependent on image selection
- **degradation_note**: With projector degradation, text on semi-transparent overlays may drop below AA if overlay opacity is too low. Recommend 70%+ opacity overlays.

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Variable - depends on execution
- **strengths**: Large text sizes provide inherent readability margin. High-contrast images (e.g., dark cityscape, bold Vietnamese flag) remain effective.
- **weaknesses**:
  > Subtle photographic details lost on projectors. Low-contrast artistic images become muddy. Text-on-image legibility degrades significantly if overlay contrast is insufficient.
- **recommendation**: Pre-test each slide's readability at reduced contrast. Use generous text overlays. Prefer high-contrast, bold imagery.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**: Excellent at the sizes this style uses. Vietnamese diacritics are never a problem at 40px+ font sizes.
- **line_height_needs**: Large sizes mean generous line-height is natural. No clipping risk.
- **minimum_safe_size**: This style's minimum (40px+) far exceeds the 28px floor needed for Vietnamese diacritics on projectors

### Cultural Fit

- **rating**: Good with careful image selection
- **analysis**:
  > Full-bleed imagery of Vietnamese landscapes, people, cultural events, and national symbols creates immediate emotional connection to the national unity topic. One-idea-per-frame approach suits Vietnamese rhetorical style which values clear, principled statements. Risk: if images are generic stock photos, the cultural connection is lost entirely.
- **adaptation_needed**: Requires curating high-quality Vietnamese-specific imagery. Generic stock photos would undermine cultural authenticity.

### Cultural Semiotics

- **achievable_symbols**:
  > Full-bleed images of: Vietnamese flag (red + gold star), ethnic minority groups in traditional dress, national landmarks (Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum, One Pillar Pagoda), rice paddies representing rural Vietnam, modern Ho Chi Minh City skyline representing development

### Content Density Capacity

## Swiss International Typographic Style

*Mathematical grid, asymmetric layouts, sans-serif hierarchy through scale/weight. Academic pedigree*

### Color Palette

- **description**:
  > Swiss Style traditionally uses a minimal, restrained palette. Color serves function (emphasis, categorization), never decoration. Classic implementations are near-monochrome with one accent.
- **background**: #FFFFFF
- **text_primary**: #000000
- **text_secondary**: #4A4A4A
- **accent_red**: #E63946
- **accent_blue**: #1D3557
- **grid_lines**: #CCCCCC
- **surface_light**: #F2F2F2
- **note**:
  > Swiss Style is not about specific colors but about how color is used: sparingly, functionally, with maximum contrast. Red is the traditional Swiss accent (Swiss flag, Basel school). For Vietnamese context, red + gold maps naturally.

### Typography

- **recommended_families**:
  - Helvetica Neue (the original Swiss Style font)
  - Inter (modern Helvetica alternative, free)
  - Be Vietnam Pro (Vietnamese-optimized, maintains Swiss geometric clarity)
- **size_scale**:
  > Hierarchy through extreme scale contrast. Display: 72-120px. Title: 48-64px. Subtitle: 32-40px. Body: 20-28px. Caption: 14-18px. The jump between levels is dramatic (2x+), not incremental.
- **weights**:
  > Bold (700) and Regular (400) only. Swiss Style achieves hierarchy through size and weight, not decoration. Light/Thin weights are used decoratively in some modern interpretations.
- **vietnamese_diacritics**:
  > Helvetica Neue has functional Vietnamese support but was not designed for it. Be Vietnam Pro maintains the geometric sans-serif clarity while being purpose-built for Vietnamese. Inter falls in between. At Swiss Style's preferred large sizes, diacritics are manageable in any of these fonts.

### Animation Style

- **approach**:
  > Minimal and geometric. Swiss Style motion follows the grid: elements slide along grid lines, scale from grid intersection points, or reveal along axes. No organic or bouncy motion.
- **entrance_patterns**:
  > Horizontal/vertical slide-ins along grid axes. Opacity fades. Clip-path reveals along geometric edges. Text typing reveals (left-to-right, following reading direction). No rotation, no bounce, no curve paths.
- **motion_budget**:
  > Low to moderate. Motion is precise and purposeful. Each animation has a clear informational purpose (revealing hierarchy, showing sequence, directing attention).
- **spring_config**: Not spring-based. Uses linear or ease-out timing functions. Duration: 300-500ms for reveals, 200-300ms for emphasis shifts.

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**: Excellent. Black text (#000000) on white (#FFFFFF) achieves 21:1 - the maximum possible contrast ratio.
- **minimum_font_sizes**:
  > Swiss Style's emphasis on large, bold typography naturally exceeds projector minimums. Display text at 72px+ and body at 20px+ (should be scaled to 28px+ for projector).
- **color_washout_resistance**:
  > Excellent. The near-monochrome palette (black/white/one accent) is inherently projector-proof. White backgrounds render perfectly on projectors. Black text remains fully legible.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **text_on_white**: 21:1 (#000000 on #FFFFFF) - maximum AAA
- **secondary_on_white**: 7.5:1 (#4A4A4A on #FFFFFF) - AAA pass
- **red_accent_on_white**: 5.2:1 (#E63946 on #FFFFFF) - AA pass for normal text, AAA for large
- **degradation_note**: With the maximum possible contrast as baseline, even severe projector degradation maintains well above AA thresholds

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Excellent
- **strengths**:
  > Maximum contrast (black on white). Grid-based layouts remain structured even when fine details blur. Large typography survives distance and degradation. Minimal color dependence means color shifts don't affect information delivery.
- **weaknesses**:
  > Thin grid lines (if visible) may disappear on projectors. Very light grays (#F2F2F2) become indistinguishable from white. Red accent may shift hue slightly.
- **recommendation**: Ensure grid lines are 2px+ if visible. Keep accent color at high saturation. The style is naturally projector-optimized.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**: Good with correct font choice. Swiss Style's large font sizes mitigate diacritics issues.
- **line_height_needs**: Swiss Style uses generous vertical spacing as a design principle. Line-height 1.4-1.6x is standard, which accommodates Vietnamese diacritics well.
- **minimum_safe_size**: 28px body text. Swiss Style typically uses 20px+ for body which should be scaled up to 28px for projector + Vietnamese diacritics.
- **note**: The mathematical grid approach means line-height and spacing are precisely calculated, preventing diacritics clipping

### Cultural Fit

- **rating**: Moderate to good
- **analysis**:
  > Swiss Style has strong academic pedigree - it was developed in universities (Basel and Zurich schools of design) and has been the visual language of academia and serious institutional communication for 70+ years. ## Vietnamese Propaganda Poster Revival

*Reinterpreted socialist-realism motifs, bold red/gold, stars, lotus. Culturally resonant with CNXHKH*

### Color Palette

- **primary**: #DA251D (Vietnamese flag red / Lebanese Red - the defining color of socialist visual culture)
- **secondary**: #FFCD00 (Vietnamese flag gold/yellow - prosperity, party symbolism)
- **accent**: #1B5E20 (revolutionary green - military, agriculture), #F5E6D0 (aged paper/poster cream)
- **surface**: #F5E6D0 (aged parchment for light mode), #FEF3C7 (warm cream for cards)
- **text**: #1C1917 (body text), #DA251D (headlines), #FFCD00 on red backgrounds (inverted for emphasis)
- **notes**:
  > Classic Vietnamese propaganda uses: red background + yellow text/stars, or cream/white background + red illustration + yellow accents. The red-gold combination is the most culturally loaded color pair in Vietnamese visual culture - it simultaneously signals national identity, Communist Party, and traditional Vietnamese aesthetics (Tet decorations, temple colors).

### Typography

- **font_families**:
  > Be Vietnam Pro (sans-serif) as primary. For poster headlines, a bolder condensed weight creates propaganda-poster feel. Consider supplementing with a Vietnamese serif for quotes/citations (though Be Vietnam Pro bold works well).
- **size_scale**:
  > Poster headline/slogan: 56-72px bold, Section title: 48px, Body: 28-32px, Citation: 24px. Propaganda style uses larger headlines than typical academic presentations.
- **weights**: 400 (body), 700 (subheadings), 800-900 (poster headlines/slogans)
- **vietnamese_diacritics_support**:
  > Critical and excellent. Vietnamese propaganda posters feature Vietnamese text prominently - diacritics are a core visual element, not an afterthought. Be Vietnam Pro at bold weights renders diacritics as integral to the letterform. At 56-72px headline size, Vietnamese diacritics become beautiful visual features.

### Animation Style

- **approach**:
  > Deliberate, authoritative motion. Elements appear with purpose, not playfulness. Fade-in with slight upward drift for solemnity. Star/lotus motifs can rotate slowly (1 revolution per 5-10 seconds). Red banners can unfurl. Spring config: damping 20-25, stiffness 60 (measured, weighty, no bounce).
- **entrance_patterns**:
  > Banner unfurl from center, text stamps into place (no overshoot), star appears with radial light burst, lotus petals open sequentially, figures/silhouettes fade in from sides
- **motion_budget**:
  > Low-moderate. Propaganda aesthetic is more about bold static composition than motion. Motion should feel ceremonial, not energetic. Key moments: flag unfurl, star reveal, slogan stamp.

### Projector Readability

- **contrast_ratio**:
  > Mixed. Red backgrounds with yellow text: ~3.5:1 (barely AA for large text). Cream background with dark text: 14:1+ (AAA). Need to use cream/light base for text-heavy slides and reserve red backgrounds for impact moments only.
- **minimum_font_sizes**: Poster headlines: 56px+ on red backgrounds (large enough to compensate for lower contrast). Body text: 28px+ on cream/light backgrounds only.
- **color_washout_resistance**:
  > High for red (saturated, survives washout well). Medium for gold/yellow (can wash out to near-white on poor projectors). Cream backgrounds are projector-friendly. Star/lotus motifs in gold need sufficient size (50px+) to remain visible.

### Wcag Contrast Score

- **text_on_background**:
  > Dark #1C1917 on cream #F5E6D0: 12.8:1 (AAA). Yellow #FFCD00 on red #DA251D: 3.2:1 (fails AA normal, borderline AA large). Red #DA251D on cream #F5E6D0: 4.2:1 (AA large text). White #FFFFFF on red #DA251D: 4.6:1 (AA).
- **recommendation**:
  > Use cream background for all text-heavy content. Red backgrounds only for short slogans at 48px+. Avoid yellow-on-red for body text (use white-on-red instead).

### Projector Degradation Resistance

- **rating**: Medium-High
- **details**:
  > Bold, saturated colors (red, gold) survive projector degradation better than pastels. High-contrast propaganda poster style (thick lines, solid fills, bold shapes) is inherently robust. However, yellow/gold can wash out to white on bright projectors - use thicker strokes and darker gold (#C7A00C) for reliability. Cream background is optimal for projectors.

### Vietnamese Diacritics Rendering

- **quality**:
  > Excellent. This style celebrates Vietnamese text as a visual element. Bold headlines at 56-72px with full diacritics (e.g., 'DAI DOAN KET TOAN DAN TOC') are the visual centerpiece. Line-height 1.4-1.5 at headline sizes. Be Vietnam Pro bold makes diacritics crisp and authoritative.

### Cultural Fit

- **assessment**:
  > Exceptional. This is the most culturally resonant option for a CNXHKH (Scientific Socialism) course presentation about national unity (dai doan ket toan dan toc). The visual language directly references Vietnamese communist party imagery, state propaganda tradition, and national symbols. It shows deep understanding of and respect for the course subject matter.
- **alignment_with_topic**:
  > Perfect - the visual style IS the topic. Vietnamese propaganda posters are the visual expression of the very ideology being studied. Using this style demonstrates engagement with the course material at a visual/cultural level, not just textual.

### Cultural Semiotics

- **symbols_invoked**:
  > Red = revolution, sacrifice, Communist Party. Gold/yellow = prosperity, national identity, five-pointed star (workers, peasants, soldiers, intellectuals, businessmen). Star = Party/national unity. Lotus = purity, Vietnamese national flower, Buddhist heritage. Green = agriculture, military. Raised fist = solidarity. Gear/rice = worker-peasant alliance.
- **risk**:
  > Could be seen as propagandistic rather than analytical if not balanced with critical content. However, for a CNXHKH course where the subject IS socialism, this is appropriate and expected. The 'revival' framing (modern reinterpretation) avoids looking like unironic propaganda.
